The Culture

  In humanitarian engineering there are a lots of external factors to ponder over. Indeed, trying to help others in the long run also means doing your utmost for your solution to fit into their everyday lives, their culture.

  Somali people are mostly muslims, hence we need to be careful to not breach any of their core beliefs.

  Although it is also reasonable to assume that our project, being a nigh-fully underground cylinder that stores water during flood, won't cause much trouble at a local-scale.

Religious Concerns

  Islam puts a lot of emphasis on the cleanliness of water which brings another reason to really pay attention to the quality of water.

  Obviously, this criterion is met as it is most important to keep the people we help in good health.

Larger Scale

  On a larger scale, the containers need to be installed in potential flooded areas. This might bring trouble as not every area would thus be eligible.

  Hence, we need to be wary to satisfy the most to not cause rivalities to arise.
